The Challenge & The Mission

Building Inclusive, Person-Centered Communities

The public policy landscape surrounding disability services is intricate. Finding the balance between high-quality support structures, Medicaid financing compliance, and long-term sustainability is a monumental task for any organization or government entity.


Potentia provides independent research, targeted systems-change strategies, and specialized training to public and private organizations. We empower you to navigate these complexities and drive transformative, sustainable results.

True reform begins with actionable intelligence.

Leadership Spotlight

C. Thomas Cook

Board President & Lead Solutions Specialist

With a robust career beginning in 1972, Thomas Cook brings unparalleled expertise to the Potentia Public Policy Institute. His distinguished background spans direct support, operational management, and specialized policy consulting for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities (I/DD).

Thomas has worked extensively as a policy analyst for the Illinois Council on Developmental Disabilities and spent over a decade consulting state and local governments on complex Medicaid rate-setting projects. His comprehensive grasp of the Brick™ Method directly fuels Potentia's mission to create fiscally responsible policies.

Meet Our Board of Directors

Guiding evidence-based policy and transformative systemic change with decades of combined experience in the public policy and human services sectors.

C. Thomas Cook

Board President & Lead Solutions Specialist

Since 1972, Thomas Cook has worked in direct support, operational, policy/design, and consulting roles in services for people with disabilities. He previously served as the executive director of the Ability Network of Delaware, successfully advocating for vital legislative funding.

His deep expertise in Medicaid rate-setting, systems-change implementation, and independent research empowers organizations across the nation to adopt fiscally responsible, high-impact strategies.

John Foote

Board Member — Behavioral Support & IDD Consultation

With over 30 years of experience in employment, workforce development, and advocacy for people with disabilities, John is a strategic thinker, skilled networker, and proven community bridge builder committed to not-for-profit transformation and systemic policy change.

John specializes in behavioral support consultation and individualized service design for adults with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD), with particular expertise in community integration and deinstitutionalization transition planning — providing consultation to organizations across the United States and Canada.

Patricia Dowse

Board Member — Workforce Development & Disability Employment

Patricia brings more than 30 years of hands-on expertise in employment advocacy, workforce skill training, and systems-change leadership for people with disabilities. A strategic thinker and accomplished networker, she has cultivated deep connections with businesses, technology companies, and national and state policy leaders.

Her work centers on forging innovative pathways to employment — partnering with disability rehabilitation providers, educators, and workforce development teams to ensure individuals with disabilities are active, employed, and fully engaged in the workforce.

Roger Deshaies

Board Member — Policy Analysis & Medicaid Strategy

Roger is a seasoned policy analyst and Medicaid strategy expert with more than seven years of leadership across JVGA and Associates and Deshaies Consulting. His work focuses on driving impactful social service transformation through evidence-based policy analysis, streamlined processes, and sustainable organizational growth.

Known for his ability to cultivate lasting partnerships and realign organizational focus, Roger ensures that complex policy initiatives are transitioned effectively — delivering long-term operational improvements for the agencies and communities he serves.
